# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Hispanic developers, computer-science / software-engineering students, and small-business owners (emprendedores) who need a working software system *now* and don't want to build it from scratch. They arrive looking for a specific stack — Python+SQLite, PHP+SQL Server, C# POS, Java, Laravel, JSP — and want proof it's real, complete, and cheap. Many are students buying a finished CRUD for a class deadline; others are founders who need a point-of-sale or admin system deployed today. They scan in Spanish, on mobile as often as desktop, and decide fast on price + perceived quality of the code.

## Product Purpose

Sin Rueda Tecnológica ("Don't reinvent the wheel") sells ready-to-use software systems and source code: download, deploy, use. The landing page is a marketing surface whose job is to (1) make the catalog instantly credible and desirable, (2) communicate the dead-simple buy→download→deploy process, and (3) drive clicks to the real store (sinruedatecnologica.com) and YouTube channel. Success = a visitor who lands, believes the code is real and worth $3–5, and clicks through to a product page. The brand promise is in the name: skip the hard part, learn to use the wheel well.

## Brand Personality

Confident, practical, builder-to-builder. Three words: **direct, capable, friendly**. The voice is a senior dev who respects your time — no fluff, no enterprise jargon, just "here's the code, here's how to run it." Warmth comes from the founder being a real, visible person (Frank Valencia) and from the playful tagline. The emotional goal: relief and trust ("oh good, someone already built this and it actually works").

## Anti-references

- The current WordPress/WooCommerce export it replaces: generic Astra theme, cramped product grid, stock-template "process" icons, no point of view.
- Generic SaaS-cream landing pages with a pastel gradient hero and three identical feature cards.
- Cheap "buy my course" infomarketer pages (countdown timers, fake scarcity, neon CTAs everywhere).
- Sterile corporate-blue agency sites. The blue here is electric and alive, not navy-and-grey.
- Editorial-magazine serif aesthetic — wrong register for a code shop.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the code, not promises.** The product covers and stack logos are the proof; let them carry the credibility. Don't describe what abstract "solutions" do — show the actual systems.
2. **Respect the buyer's speed.** Price, stack, and a clear "Ver producto" should be visible at a glance. Two clicks to the store, max.
3. **Builder-to-builder honesty.** Copy talks like a developer to a developer. No marketing inflation; the $3 price IS the pitch.
4. **The wheel is the brand.** Lean into "don't reinvent the wheel" as the organizing idea — the founder, the tagline, the mark all reinforce it.
5. **Dark is home, light is welcome.** The product art is dark-native; the dark theme is the hero, but a real light theme ships too (user choice, respected and persisted).

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥4.5:1 in both themes; large text ≥3:1. Full keyboard navigation, visible focus rings, semantic landmarks, alt text in Spanish on every image. Theme toggle persists and respects `prefers-color-scheme`. All motion has a `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative. Spanish (es) as the document language.
